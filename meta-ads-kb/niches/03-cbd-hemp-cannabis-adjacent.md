# 03 — CBD / Hemp / Cannabis-adjacent

The single hardest e-com niche to advertise on Meta. Most brands run paid social on the wrong side of the policy line.

## What's allowed (Meta policy as of 2025–2026)

✅ Allowed (US only, with conditions):
- **Topical CBD** (creams, lotions, salves, beauty / skincare with CBD) — *if* the brand is **LegitScript-certified** AND has **written authorization from Meta** AND targets **18+** (some states 21+)
- **Non-CBD hemp** (hemp seed, hemp fiber, hemp seed oil — non-psychoactive, no CBD) — allowed in US, Canada, Mexico without written authorization
- LegitScript certification: https://www.legitscript.com/

✅ Allowed (without certification, with constraints):
- Hemp clothing
- Hempcrete / industrial hemp materials
- Pure CBD beauty / skincare (topical, no claims, certified)

## What's banned

❌ Always banned:
- **Ingestible CBD** (gummies, tinctures, capsules, drinks, edibles)
- Anything with **THC** (Delta-8, Delta-9, Delta-10, HHC, THC-O, THC-P, all variants)
- **Cannabis** in any form (including state-legal)
- "Hemp gummies" / "hemp tinctures" sold for ingestion (regardless of cannabinoid content)
- Anything with **health claims** ("relieves anxiety", "helps you sleep", "treats pain")
- **Targeting users under 18** (or under 21 in some states)
- CBD ads in **any country other than the US**

## What gets disapproved despite being "allowed"

⚠️ Frequently flagged:
- Topical CBD ads where copy implies effect ("calms inflammation" — depends on phrasing)
- Hemp-seed-oil ads that look identical to CBD ads
- LegitScript-certified brands that drift in copy ("our CBD oil...")
- Any ad mentioning CBD by name without written Meta authorization
- Ads with cannabis leaves visible (even hemp-seed-oil ads)

## Public-scraping red flags

| Red flag | Severity |
|---|---|
| Brand sells ingestible CBD/THC and runs Meta ads | Critical (account-level review imminent) |
| Targeting outside US | Critical |
| Health claims in copy ("relieves anxiety", "for pain") | Critical |
| Cannabis leaf imagery | High |
| Missing LegitScript badge on LP | High (if running CBD topicals) |
| No 18+/21+ age gate on LP | High |
| Implied medical claim | High |
| "Hemp" branding while clearly selling CBD | Medium |
| Influencer content showing the product being ingested | Critical |

## Landing-page checks

| Element | Required? |
|---|---|
| LegitScript certification badge (visible) | Yes (CBD topicals) |
| Age-gate on entry (18+/21+) | Yes |
| THC content stated (<0.3% THC, or "0.0% THC") | Yes |
| State-by-state shipping restrictions | Yes (sale restrictions vary widely) |
| Product disclaimers ("not for use under 18", "consult physician if pregnant…") | Yes |
| FDA disclaimer ("not evaluated by the FDA") | Yes |

## Creative norms that work (within the narrow allowed lane)

- Topical / spa / wellness aesthetic (no leaves)
- Ingredient-led (when CBD isn't the lead ingredient)
- Hemp-seed-oil under "wellness oil" framing
- Founder / origin story, no claims
- Sport-recovery angle for topicals (sore muscles, no medical claim)
- LegitScript / certification-led trust building

## What doesn't work / gets killed

- Anything anxiety / sleep / pain in copy
- Cannabis leaf or marijuana / weed iconography
- "Wellness benefits" lists that include therapeutic claims
- "Natural alternative to [medical product]"
- Any "before / after" related to symptoms
- Influencer testimonials about effects

## Niche reality check

The brutal truth, which the audit needs to convey diplomatically: the *vast majority* of CBD/hemp brands that "run Meta ads" are doing it through:
1. Hemp-seed-oil / topical brand pages that boost organic posts to skirt review
2. Paid partnerships disguised as influencer organic
3. Splitting their ad spend to TikTok/Google/Programmatic where CBD policy is more flexible

If the brand is in this category, the cold-audit insight isn't "your ads break policy" — it's "your customer acquisition is under-diversified because Meta cuts you off at scale; here's how to architect around it."

## Sample-report wording template

> **Compliance — CBD / Hemp**
> Your brand is in one of Meta's hardest enforcement categories. Your active ads use language that historically triggers takedowns:
> - Ad `<id>`: `<quote>` — therapeutic claim, will be flagged.
> - Ad `<id>`: cannabis-leaf imagery — even on hemp-product ads, this gets clustered with cannabis enforcement.
> Beyond the immediate policy fixes, the structural issue is *concentration risk*: a single Meta account-level review can pause your acquisition for weeks. We've architected around this with [your service] in [vertical] — happy to walk you through it on a call.
