# 12 — Pet Products

Mostly permissive. The "pet supplements" sub-niche follows supplement rules; the rest is open.

## Hard rules

✅ Allowed:
- Pet food / treats / chews / toys
- Pet apparel
- Pet accessories (collars, beds, bowls)
- Pet tech (cameras, GPS trackers, automated feeders)
- Subscription boxes (BarkBox, Chewy auto-ship style)
- Pet insurance (own restrictions — see below)
- Pet training programs / books

❌ Banned:
- Live animals (sale or transfer of pets)
- Counterfeit pet products
- Health claims without veterinary backing
- "Cures" / "treats" pet medical conditions
- Aggressive flea / tick poison framing
- Counterfeit / off-brand prescription parallels

⚠️ Restricted:
- **Pet supplements** — follow human supplement rules (no medical claims)
- **Pet pharmaceuticals** (flea/tick prescription, joint Rx, anxiety meds) — vet-prescription-only, restricted
- **CBD pet products** — same restrictions as human CBD (LegitScript-only, US-only)
- **Pet insurance** — finance-adjacent, may need disclosures

## Pet supplement rules

Same as human supplements:
- No "treats / cures / heals"
- No specific outcome promises ("eliminates joint pain")
- No before/after with the pet (often flagged as body-image-adjacent enforcement)
- "Supports joint health" is the safe alternative
- Need disclaimer if making any health adjacent claim

## Pet CBD

Same as human CBD:
- LegitScript certification
- Topical only (no ingestible CBD)
- US-only
- 18+ targeting
- No therapeutic claims for the pet

## Public-scraping red flags

| Red flag | Severity |
|---|---|
| "Cures [pet condition]" copy | Critical |
| Imagery of distressed / sick / scared pet | High |
| Before/after on supplement / vet-adjacent | High |
| Live-animal sale | Critical |
| Veterinary credential without naming the vet | Medium |
| "Vet-recommended" without specifics | Medium |
| Aggressive emotional manipulation ("save your pet's life!") | Medium-High |
| Pet CBD outside US | Critical |
| Implied prescription replacement | High |

## Landing-page checks

| Element | Required for |
|---|---|
| Ingredient list | Food / supplements / treats |
| Allergen disclosures | Food / treats |
| Veterinary advisor / endorsement (if claimed) | Supplements / health-adjacent |
| Safety / non-toxic certifications | Toys, beds, food |
| Recall history (food) | Food / treats |
| Sourcing / origin | Food / treats |
| Sizing / breed-suitability | Apparel, beds, harnesses |
| Returns / freshness guarantee | Subscription |

## Creative norms that work

- Pet UGC / pet-influencer content (highest engagement on Meta in this category)
- Founder / pet-parent storytelling
- "Day in the life" of the pet using the product
- Mechanism content for tech / training products
- Comparison vs. drugstore alternatives
- Subscription / convenience framing
- Veterinary endorsement (with named, credentialed vet)
- Heritage / "trusted for N years" for established brands
- Adoption / rescue partnership angles

## What doesn't work / gets killed

- Distressed / sick pet imagery (penalty + low CTR)
- Stock pet imagery (low engagement)
- Generic "your pet deserves better" framing
- Aggressive scarcity / fear-based copy
- Implied medical effect on the pet without backing

## Sample-report wording template

> **Compliance — Pet (with supplement sub-product)**
> Two findings on the supplement product line:
> - Ad `<id>` claims `Eliminates joint pain in 7 days` — pet supplements follow the same restrictions as human supplements; "treats / eliminates" is a banned phrasing. Compliant rewrite: `Supports healthy joint mobility, formulated for senior dogs.`
> - Your supplement LP at `<url>` is missing the disclaimer "Not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease" — required when health-adjacent claims appear with a CTA.
