# 10 — Apparel / Fashion

The most-permissive Shopify e-com vertical for Meta — but copyright / trademark is the silent killer.

## Hard rules

❌ Banned:
- Counterfeit / replica products (Gucci, LV, Nike, etc.)
- "Inspired by [trademarked brand]" — flagged as counterfeit-adjacent
- Unauthorized character / IP licensing (Disney, Marvel, sports teams)
- Sexually explicit imagery (lingerie has narrow allowances)
- Body-shape / fit-shaming copy ("look thinner", "hide your belly")
- Faux body-image transformation (shapewear with extreme before/after)

⚠️ Restricted:
- Sportswear that crosses into supplements / wellness claims
- "As seen on [celebrity]" without licensing
- Shapewear / body-shaping with weight-loss-adjacent copy
- Lingerie / swimwear modeling rules (close-up imagery)

✅ Allowed:
- Apparel / fashion ads in any region (with regional taste differences)
- Brand-comparison / "vs. [competitor]" copy when factually true
- Style / lifestyle imagery
- UGC / influencer (with proper paid-partnership tags)
- Subscription / drop-style scarcity (real, not artificial)

## Specific creative concerns

### Lingerie / swimwear

Meta's policy: imagery must show garment "in context" — fully styled, not isolated close-up. The line is enforced inconsistently.

- ✅ Person wearing the lingerie in lifestyle / styled context
- ⚠️ Lingerie on a model in plain studio shot (often flagged)
- ❌ Close-up of a body part wearing lingerie (banned)

### Shapewear

Borderline weight-loss. Compliant framing is "smooth silhouette" / "comfort fit", not "look thinner" / "hide your belly".

### Sustainability / "ethical fashion" claims

Specific claims need substantiation:
- "Made from recycled X%" — yes, with %
- "Carbon-neutral" — needs offsetting partner / certification
- "Ethically made" — vague; Meta may not enforce, but FTC guidelines tightening 2025
- "Sustainable" alone — too vague; flag if the only claim

## Public-scraping red flags

| Red flag | Severity |
|---|---|
| "Inspired by [brand name]" | Critical (counterfeit-adjacent) |
| Unauthorized character imagery | Critical |
| Body-shape framing on shapewear | High |
| Lingerie close-up imagery | Medium-High |
| Vague "sustainable" claim with no spec | Medium |
| "As seen on [celebrity]" without authorization | High |
| Replica / dupe framing | Critical |
| Influencer ads missing paid-partnership tag | Medium |

## Landing-page checks

- Sizing chart (mandatory for apparel)
- Returns / exchanges policy clear and reasonable
- Shipping / delivery times stated
- Material composition listed
- Country of origin (for "Made in" claims)
- Privacy policy + terms

## Creative norms that work

- Lifestyle / aesthetic ("the new fall capsule")
- Founder / brand-story content
- "Behind the seams" — manufacturing / craft footage
- UGC styling content ("how I wore the [piece]")
- Bundle / capsule curation
- Drop / launch scarcity (when real)
- Color story / seasonal trend
- Customer review videos / unboxings
- Sport / activity context for activewear

## What doesn't work / gets killed

- Generic studio product shots in feed (low CTR)
- Heavy text overlays (text-density penalty)
- "20% off" stickers slapped on every ad (Meta soft-throttles spam-style ads)
- Same product, four-pose carousel (one-concept trap)
- Body-shaming framing
- Counterfeit / dupe positioning

## Sample-report wording template

> **Compliance — Apparel**
> Apparel is one of Meta's most permissive e-com verticals; your issues are creative-architecture, not compliance:
> - Eight of your nine active ads are studio product shots (single concept lane). Andromeda's 2025 retrieval engine clusters semantically-similar ads — so functionally you're running one "concept" across nine ads. Concept-count target for your spend bracket is 5–8 distinct angles.
> - Two ads use the phrase `Inspired by [iconic brand name]`. Even when the product isn't a counterfeit, that phrasing is in Meta's counterfeit-detection word-list and slows delivery on the entire ad set.
